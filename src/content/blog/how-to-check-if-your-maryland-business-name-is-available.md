## How to Search the SDAT Database

Start at the <a href="https://egov.maryland.gov/businessexpress/entitysearch" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">Maryland Department of Assessments and Taxation (SDAT) Business Entity Search</a>. This is the state's official database of registered businesses.

Navigate to the <a href="https://egov.maryland.gov/businessexpress/entitysearch" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">SDAT website</a> and click "Business Entity Search." Search options include Department ID, business name, resident agent, and address.

Search by business name.

### Search Strategy That Actually Works

The search is sensitive to formatting. Punctuation matters.

Commas, periods, and hyphens surface different results even when the underlying business name should theoretically match. This is the detail most guides skip.

Use the "Contains" option instead of "Exact Match." This catches variations you'd miss with a narrow search.

Search your desired name without punctuation first. Then search again with common variations.

If you want "Summit Solutions LLC," search for "Summit Solutions" and also try "Summit" alone to see what else exists in that namespace.

The system updates Monday through Friday during business hours. Recent filings might not appear immediately. Give it a day or two if timing is tight.

When you find results, click through to the charter details. You'll see registered agent information, filing history, and current status. Pay attention to businesses in similar industries or with similar naming patterns.

**Key point:** The "Contains" search option catches formatting variations the database won't surface with exact match searches. Punctuation differences create false negatives.

## How to Evaluate Similar Names

A clean "no results" answer is rare. Most searches return something adjacent.

The question becomes: is this similar name a problem?

Maryland has discretion here.

The state rejects filings if your name is "confusingly similar" to an existing entity, even when it's not an exact match. This determination happens during the filing process, not during your search.

That's the gap most people miss.

Your name clears the database search but still gets rejected by the clerk reviewing your paperwork.

### Framework for Evaluating Risk

Check if the existing business operates in your industry.

"Summit Consulting LLC" doing IT work creates higher confusion risk with "Summit Solutions LLC" also doing IT work than if one was a bakery.

Look at the distinctive elements.

Generic descriptors like "Consulting," "Solutions," "Services," or "Group" create minimal separation. If the only difference between your name and an existing business is swapping one generic term for another, you're in risky territory.

Consider phonetic similarities.

Names that sound alike when spoken trigger rejections even if they're spelled differently.

If you're unsure, pick a more distinctive name. The cost of getting rejected and starting over outweighs the attachment to a marginal choice.

**Key point:** Maryland clerks have discretion to reject names that are confusingly similar, even when database searches show availability. Industry overlap and phonetic similarity create the highest rejection risk.

## What Are Maryland's LLC Naming Requirements?

Maryland has specific rules about what your LLC name must include and what it prohibits.

### Required Designations

Your LLC name must include one of these designations:

- "Limited Liability Company"
- "LLC"
- "L.L.C."

The business name alone won't work. "Summit Solutions" gets rejected. "Summit Solutions LLC" passes.

### Restricted Words

Restricted words require additional licensing or approval.

Words like "bank," "insurance," "university," or "trust" trigger regulatory scrutiny. If your business name includes these terms, you'll need to prove you have the appropriate licenses before Maryland approves the name.

The state also prohibits names that imply government affiliation or mislead the public about your business structure.

**Key point:** Every Maryland LLC name must include "LLC," "L.L.C.," or "Limited Liability Company." Restricted words like "bank" or "insurance" require proof of appropriate licensing before approval.

## How to Check Federal Trademarks

The SDAT search only tells you if your name conflicts with Maryland business registrations.

It doesn't tell you if you're stepping on someone's trademark.

Trademark protection operates separately from business registration. A company has trademark rights to a name even if they're not registered as a business entity in Maryland.

Navigate to the <a href="https://tmsearch.uspto.gov/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">USPTO Trademark Electronic Search System (TESS)</a>. Search for your desired business name and variations of it.

Look for exact matches and similar names in your industry classification.

Trademarks are categorized by goods and services classes. A trademark for "Summit Solutions" in software might not conflict with "Summit Solutions" in landscaping, but you need to verify.

This step prevents the expensive problem.

Proper trademark clearance costs $500 to $1,500 and takes six to eight weeks. <a href="https://www.frozenlemons.com/blog/business-naming-mistakes-cost-startups-millions/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">Trademark litigation averages</a> $120,000 to $750,000 per case.

The math is clear. Spend the time on the search.

**Key point:** State business registration and federal trademark protection operate independently. USPTO TESS searches prevent trademark infringement claims that average $120,000 to $750,000 in litigation costs.

## Why Domain Availability Matters

Check domain availability before you commit to the name.

Navigate to any domain registrar and search for your business name with common extensions (.com, .net, .org).

If the .com is available, register it immediately. Wait until after you file your LLC paperwork and you create an opening.

Some companies monitor USPTO trademark filings and purchase matching domains to resell at higher prices. Securing your domain before you file prevents this extraction.

If the exact match domain isn't available, you have options.

Add a geographic modifier (summitsolutionsmd.com), use a different extension, or reconsider the name entirely.

Domain availability often signals broader name conflicts. If the .com is taken by a business in your industry, you probably have a trademark problem even if the SDAT search came back clean.

**Key point:** Secure your .com domain before filing LLC paperwork to prevent domain squatting. Domain unavailability in your industry often indicates trademark conflicts the SDAT search won't reveal.

## Should You Reserve or File Immediately?

Maryland offers name reservation for $25. This holds your business name for 30 days while you prepare your LLC formation documents.

When does reservation make sense?

### Reserve the Name If

You need time to finalize your operating agreement, gather member information, or complete other formation requirements. The reservation prevents someone else from taking your name while you handle the administrative details.

You're validating market demand before committing fully. You want the name secured but you're not ready to file the full LLC yet.

You found a strong name and you're concerned about losing it in the time it takes to get everything else ready.

### Skip the Reservation and File Immediately If

You have all your formation documents ready. There's no benefit to paying $25 for a reservation when you file the Articles of Organization and secure the name permanently.

Speed matters more than perfection. Getting to market quickly beats waiting for the ideal setup. You refine and even rebrand later if needed.

You've validated that the name works and you're ready to move forward.

The reservation is insurance, not a requirement. Use it when the timing justifies the cost.

**Key point:** Name reservation ($25, 30 days) makes sense when you need prep time. Skip it and file Articles of Organization immediately when you're ready to secure the name permanently.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does the Maryland SDAT search take?

The actual search takes 5 to 10 minutes. Searching variations and evaluating similar names adds another 10 to 15 minutes. Total time is under 30 minutes for thorough validation.

No. Maryland clerks have discretion to reject names they determine are confusingly similar, even when the database search shows availability. Phonetic similarity and industry overlap create rejection risk.

### Do I need to check trademarks if my name clears the SDAT search?

Yes. State business registration and federal trademark protection operate independently. A USPTO search prevents infringement claims from businesses operating outside Maryland.

### What happens if I file without checking trademarks first?

### Should I register my domain before or after filing my LLC?

### How much does Maryland name reservation cost?

### What's the difference between "Contains" and "Exact Match" in SDAT search?

"Contains" catches formatting variations (punctuation, spacing) the database won't surface with exact matches. Commas, periods, and hyphens create different search results for the same underlying name.

### Will my LLC name work if the .com domain is taken?

Legally, yes. Practically, domain unavailability in your industry often signals trademark conflicts. Geographic modifiers (summitsolutionsmd.com) or different extensions (.net, .org) work, but reconsider if a direct competitor owns the .com.
